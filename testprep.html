Which Test Is Right for You?
SAT vs ACT

Both are accepted by every college in the US. The best strategy is to take a free diagnostic for each and go with the one that feels more natural.

Know What's Tested
Subject Breakdown

Every section has a predictable set of topics. Knowing exactly what's tested lets you focus your time instead of studying everything.

📐
Math
SAT & ACT · Heaviest section weight

Key Topics

  • Algebra I & II (linear equations, systems, inequalities)
  • Geometry (area, angles, triangles, circles)
  • Basic trigonometry (SAT: sin/cos/tan; ACT: more trig)
  • Data analysis & statistics
  • Word problems & modeling
  • ACT only: matrices, logarithms, sequences

  • Sentence structure & clarity
  • Reading comprehension: main idea, evidence, tone

Science
ACT only · No memorization required

What It Actually Tests

  • Data interpretation (reading tables, graphs, charts)
  • Experimental design & research summaries
  • Conflicting viewpoints (compare two scientists' claims)
  • Logical reasoning — not bio, chem, or physics knowledge
  • Speed: 40 questions in 35 minutes = tight pacing

Sophomore Prep Timeline

No need to panic — you have more runway than most. This timeline keeps prep realistic around your soccer season.

Now — End of Sophomore Year
Take Diagnostics & Find Your Baseline
No pressure, just data. Take one free SAT diagnostic on Khan Academy and one free ACT practice test from ACT.org. Compare your scores and how each felt. No studying needed beforehand — you want an honest baseline.
Khan Academy SAT Diagnostic ACT.org Free Practice Test Identify Weakest Section
Summer Before Junior Year
Light Daily Study — Build the Foundation
No tournaments, less pressure. Commit to 30 minutes a day on your weakest subject. Khan Academy math lessons or Meltzer grammar rules. Read the PrepScholar blog to understand the test structure. This summer compound-interests your prep — it pays off big in the fall.
30 min/day Khan Academy 1 practice section/week Read PrepScholar strategy guides
Junior Fall (Sept – Dec)
Full Practice Tests & Targeted Review
One full practice test per month under timed conditions. Review every wrong answer — understand why you missed it, not just what the right answer was. Take the PSAT in October — it's free, low-stakes, and gives you official College Board data on your performance.
Monthly full practice test PSAT in October Start Meltzer grammar book
Junior Spring (Jan – May)
First Real Test Attempt
Register early — test centers fill up. Take both SAT and ACT once if possible. These are your exploratory attempts — scores still count and many colleges accept them, but you'll have more shots. Consider one month of Magoosh as a sprint in the month before your test.
Register by January Take SAT (March or May) Take ACT (April) Optional: 1-month Magoosh
Junior Summer (June – Aug)
Analyze & Retake if Needed
Review your score reports — both College Board and ACT provide section-level breakdowns. If you're within range of your goal, keep the score. If not, targeted prep on weak sections and retake in June or July.
Analyze score reports Retake if 50+ points from goal Send scores to target schools
Senior Fall (Aug – Nov)
Final Attempt if Needed
Most colleges want scores by November for regular decision. If you hit your target junior year, you're done — focus on your application. If not, one more attempt in August or October. Coaches are typically engaged by this point and may have already waived score requirements for soccer recruits.

Smart Strategy
Quick Tips

Things Most Students Don't Know

📅
Register early. Popular test dates fill up fast — especially March and October SAT and April ACT. Register 6–8 weeks out to get your preferred location.
🔄
SAT Superscoring works in your favor. College Board combines your best section scores across multiple test dates. Take it twice and submit both — they'll build the best composite for you automatically.
⚽
Coaches care most about GPA and soccer tape. Test scores are secondary for athletic recruits — but academic scholarships often have score floors (usually ACT 23+ / SAT 1100+), so hitting the threshold still matters for your financial aid package.
📄
Only official tests are worth simulating. Third-party practice tests (from most prep books) are slightly off in difficulty. When doing full timed simulations, use official College Board or ACT.org tests only.
🎯
Wrong answers teach more than right ones. After every practice test, spend as much time reviewing missed questions as you did taking the test. Understand the pattern — most students miss the same question types repeatedly.
💤
Sleep beats last-minute cramming. The night before the test, stop studying after a light review. Sleep is the single biggest performance variable on test day — especially if you've been prepping consistently for months.
